Why UNIT Protocol

Simple structure. Clear rules. Fully on‑chain.

Most tokens fail because they are built around promises that cannot be verified. UNIT Protocol keeps things intentionally simple: a clean BEP‑20 contract, transparent allocation and a roadmap focused on real, auditable utility — not speculation narratives.

01 · Transparent

Verifiable on‑chain from day one

Contract code is published on BscScan, total supply is fixed at deployment, and all movements of tokens happen directly on the blockchain. Anyone can independently verify balances, transfers and allocation.

02 · Utility‑first

Designed to plug into products

UNIT is meant to be integrated into real tools — loyalty systems, gated content, governance, payment rails and reward programs — with a focus on legal, compliant and transparent usage.

03 · Risk‑aware

No guaranteed returns, ever

UNIT does not promise price appreciation, dividends or profit sharing. It is a digital tool and access token. The market is free to price it as it wishes; participants choose their own level of risk.

How to buy

Get UNIT safely on BNB Smart Chain.

The steps below describe a generic, non‑custodial way to access UNIT. Always check that you are using the correct contract address and that you understand how self‑custody works before sending funds.

1

Set up a BSC wallet

Install a trusted non‑custodial wallet (for example MetaMask) and add the BNB Smart Chain network. Securely back up your Seed / Secret Recovery Phrase offline. Never share it with anyone.

2

Fund with BNB

Acquire a small amount of BNB on a reputable exchange and withdraw it to your BSC wallet address. You will need BNB to pay network transaction fees and, if using a DEX, to swap into UNIT.

3

Swap for UNIT

Use a DEX that supports BNB Smart Chain. Import the UNIT token contract 0x4f5710c96C446b133C59Af006A366d07414B52a9, double‑check every character, then swap a chosen amount of BNB or a supported token into UNIT.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

A short overview of how UNIT is positioned. More detailed, jurisdiction‑specific information should always be taken from local professionals and regulators.

Is UNIT an investment?

No. UNIT is a digital utility and access token. While it can be traded on secondary markets, there are no promises of profit, dividends or capital protection. Price is entirely determined by open markets.

Who controls UNIT?

UNIT is deployed as a smart contract on BNB Smart Chain. Once ownership is renounced and parameters are locked according to the contract, there is no central entity that can unilaterally change supply or seize user funds. Community coordination happens transparently on‑chain and across public channels.

Can the rules change in the future?

Smart contract rules are enforced by the blockchain and cannot be changed unless explicitly coded to do so. Off‑chain policies, such as how applications integrate UNIT, may evolve to stay aligned with security best practices and regulatory guidance.

Do I need KYC to hold UNIT?

Holding UNIT in a self‑custodial wallet typically does not require KYC. However, exchanges, fiat on‑ramps, off‑ramps and certain jurisdictions may apply KYC/AML requirements. Always follow the rules of the platforms and countries you use.
